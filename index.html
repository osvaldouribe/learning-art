Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci created the majority of his work during the 15th and 16th centuries. His artistic career spanned several decades, with notable periods including his time in Florence, Milan, and later in France. Da Vinci's most significant works were produced between approximately 1470 and 1519. During this time, he created masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa, The Last Supper, and The Vitruvian Man, among many others. Da Vinci's artistic output encompassed not only painting but also sculpture, architecture, anatomy studies, engineering designs, and scientific investigations. His work continues to be revered for its innovation, beauty, and enduring influence on the art world.

Pablo Picasso

He produced a significant portion of his work during the 20th century. His artistic career spanned several decades, and he was highly prolific, creating artworks across various styles and periods. Picasso's most renowned periods include the Blue Period (1901-1904), Rose Period (1904-1906), African art-inspired Period (1907-1909), Analytic Cubism (1909-1912), and Synthetic Cubism (1912-1919). However, he continued to produce art throughout his life, experimenting with different styles and mediums until his death in 1973.

Fernando Botero

a renowned Colombian artist known for his distinctive style characterized by voluminous figures, has created a vast body of work throughout his career. The majority of Botero's work was produced from the 1960s onwards. During this period, he developed and perfected his signature style, often depicting subjects such as people, animals, and still life objects with exaggerated proportions. Botero's career has spanned several decades, and he continues to create art to this day, making him one of the most prominent and prolific artists of the 20th and 21st centuries.
